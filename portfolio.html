PLTW 1.1.9 Project - An Imaginary World

I used the Python Turtle to draw a scene of a house. I collected input from the user at the beginning, so that you can choose what color you want the house, the roof, the door, the windows, the flowers, the ladybug, and the spider to be. At the end, the user gets to choose what flying object they would like to appear - a choice between a plane, a bee or a hot air balloon.

PLTW 1.2.5 Project - I Made This Up On The Fly

My team members and I coded a game called "I Made This Up On The Fly." The objective of the game is to squish as many bugs as you can in 30 seconds. When you run the game, an image of a fly appears. When you click on the fly, a math question shows up. If you type the correct answer to the math problem, you get one point and you move on to the next bug. The image of the fly turns into a dead troll face. If you get a question wrong, then text appears, saying "lol ur dumb." You keep playing until the 30 seconds are over. We made a list of coordinates and randomized them so that the fly appears in different places. We also included a timer. At the end of the 30 seconds, you there is text saying "Congratulations, you got _ correct out of _."
