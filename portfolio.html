PLTW 1.1.9 Project - An Imaginary World



I used the Python Turtle to draw a scene of a house. I collected input from the user at the beginning, so that you can choose

what color you want the house, the roof, the door, the windows, the flowers, the ladybug, and the spider to be.

At the end, the user gets to choose what flying object they would like to appear - a choice between a plane, a bee or a hot air balloon.




PLTW 1.2.5 Project - I Made This Up On The Fly


My team members and I coded a game called "I Made This Up On The Fly." The objective of the game is to squish

as many bugs as you can in 30 seconds. When you run the game, an image of a fly appears. When you click on the fly,

a math question shows up. If you type the correct answer to the math problem, you get one point and you move on to the

next bug. The image of the fly turns into a dead troll face. If you get a question wrong, then text appears, saying "lol ur dumb."

You keep playing until the 30 seconds are over. We made a list of coordinates and randomized them so that the fly appears in different

places. We also included a timer. At the end of the 30 seconds, it tells you how many problems you got correct.




PLTW 1.3.1 Project - Lost in the Woods, Interactive Story


For our third project in this class my group and I decided to create a choose-your-own-adventure interactive story. In our story,

you go into the woods to explore, but you end up getting lost. You're trying to find your way back home. On your journey, you are

given choices, and those choices will affect whether you live or die. In our story there are references to fairy tales, such as

Goldilocks and the Three Bears and Hansel and Gretel. At the end of the game, whether or not you win or lose, you are given the choice

to either start over or just close the game. Some details about our code is that at the beginning you are asked what gender you'd like

to be and what your name is. Your name pops up at the beginning when the game tells you it is ready to begin. The gender you choose

determines which character gif you are for the whole story, either boy or girl. When you move from one decision to the next, the background

changes, and your character "walks" from one scene to the next. Overall it was a very fun project!
